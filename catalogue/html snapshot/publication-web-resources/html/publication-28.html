This composition immerses the viewer in a rich interplay of light and shadow, where the cool, saturated blues and layered textures evoke a sense of otherworldly transformation. The fragmented forms of the figure blend with abstract patterns, creating a dynamic tension between corporeal presence and ethereal abstraction. The spine, illuminated and central, acts as a visual anchor, suggesting strength and vulnerability in equal measure.

The use of vivid color disrupts the muted tones typical of the series, introducing a sensory intensity that shifts the work’s emotional register. The overlapping limbs and textured surfaces create a kaleidoscopic effect, as though the figure is both dis-solving and coalescing within the composition. This simultaneity of movement and stillness deepens the work’s exploration of embodiment as a transient and multifaceted experience.

Within bodyTime(fugitivity), this piece situates the body as both subject and vessel, holding traces of touch, light, and time. The interplay of the physical and the abstract invites the viewer to navigate the liminal space where identity, form, and per-ception blur. It is a meditation on transformation—on the body as both a site of presence and a canvas for the unseen forces that shape it.
