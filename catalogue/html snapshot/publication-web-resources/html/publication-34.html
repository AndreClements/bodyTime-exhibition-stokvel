20230917_1 (Refraction)

This vivid composition intensifies the dialogue between presence and multiplicity by employing a mirror as both backdrop and conceptual anchor. The reflective surface fragments the figure, creating a layered interplay of identity and self-perception. Saturated reds and stark shadows amplify the tension, rendering the subject both intimate and distant, as if caught within the dualities of introspection and external observation.

The tattooed arm, illuminated with dramatic contrast, anchors the figure in tangible reality, while the mirrored doubling introduces a distortion that challenges singular notions of self. The hand-to-mouth gesture hints at contemplation or restraint, yet the fractured layers suggest a struggle to reconcile inner thoughts with outward form. The mirror, as both a literal and metaphorical device, refracts not just light but identity itself.
