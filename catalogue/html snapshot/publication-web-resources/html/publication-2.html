bodyTime(fugitivity)

Introduction

The 16 works in bodyTime(fugitivity) explore time, embodiment, and relationality through layered compositions that merge multiple temporalities. André Clements utelises the photographic act—not as a singular capture but as a generative recursive unfolding. Each piece resists the traditional focus on an instant, on a rendering, instead layering dual moments to reveal the fragmented and transient essence of being. These works are thresholds, inviting viewers into the space between presence and absence, intimacy and abstraction. They are not conclusions but points of departure into shifting perceptions of self and time. The title, drawn from Object-Oriented programming syntax, frames bodyTime() as a mutable function rather than a static object. Here, fugitivity—life’s intrinsic impermanence—becomes a dynamic parameter. Through this lens, each image reflects the interplay of movement, touch, and gaze, inscribing temporal and emotional complexity onto the body. This computational metaphor deepens the series’ philosophical foundation, drawing on Object-Ori-ented Ontology (OOO) to reject hierarchical binaries of subject and object in favor of multiplicity and relationality.Presented as part of the Go Naked naturist stand at The Sex Expo under the theme “nonsexual nudity in a sexualized world,” the series directly challenges the commodification of nudity. These works reclaim the nude body as a site of transforma-tion and vulnerability, resisting objectification and offering instead a layered exploration of presence, memory, and touch. Nudity here is neither purely erotic nor purely aesthetic but a point of intersection between time and embodied experience.Rooted in historical and contemporary artistic traditions, bodyTime(fugitivity) echoes Duchamp’s fractured temporality in Nude Descending a Staircase and Muybridge’s motion studies, while introducing a recursive, digital dimension. Each work invites viewers to reflect on how time, gaze, and memory shape the way we see and are seen. In a world that reduces the body to symbol or commodity, this series insists on its complexity—calling us to pause, reconsider, and engage with the layered essence of being.
